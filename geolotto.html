GeoLotto
Our innovative draw game utilising the power of Google Maps

{% include sales-cta-well.html %}

Pick a place, any place

We use a map of your lottery region and divide it into squares which we call 'Geos'. Each Geo acts as a lottery ticket. Players navigate the map to find places of significance to them – their old school, their university or their holiday home.

Engage a new generation

Our fun and familiar Google Maps game-board is a smart reinvention of lottery gaming, allowing operators to reach out to new players.

How to play

1. Pick a lucky place

  • Players are shown a Google Map divided into squares called Geos
  • Clicking a vacant Geo allows the player to buy it
  • Players can choose any Geo they like: their home, a landmark, their football club, their workplace... whatever they fancy!
  • The purchase of one Geo gives the player one entry in the draw
  • Purple Geos show players where prizes were claimed in the last draw

2. Register & deposit

  • New players are asked to register their details before they can play
  • Upon registration, players can make a deposit of any amount and make multiple entries into the prize draw
  • Players can also occupy their lucky Geo using a weekly subscription

3. Enter the draw

  • At the end of the draw period, players are notified by email if they've won or they can log in to their account to see their results
  • Every day, millions of Geos can yield winning prizes
  • Prizes can range from small amounts to multi-million jackpots
  • Game odds, prize levels and draw periods can be configured to the operator's requirements

Features

Multi-platform gaming

GeoLotto runs in any standard web browser and is widely accessible on mobile devices including including the iPhone and iPad as well as Android.

Social media integration

Players can easily connect with social media tools like Facebook and Twitter to invite friends to play and boast about their wins!

Flexible draw structure

Utilising the power of our hardware random number generator, operators can configure odds and prize structures with maximum flexibility.

Game board configuration

Lotteries can choose any number of Geos to place on the map and vary their size. Imagine compensating for different population densities to give your players a better chance of winning!
